About Stephen Curry

Stephen Curry, born March 14, 1988, in Akron, Ohio, is a professional basketball player for the Golden State Warriors. Widely regarded as the greatest shooter in NBA history, Curry revolutionized the game with his deep three-point shooting. His incredible shooting range, combined with his ball-handling skills and leadership, has redefined how basketball is played. Curry is also a multiple-time NBA champion, two-time MVP, and has set numerous records throughout his career.

Despite being overlooked by many major colleges due to his size, Curry made his mark in college basketball at Davidson, leading his team to the NCAA Tournament's Elite Eight in 2008. His college success led to him being selected as the 7th overall pick in the 2009 NBA Draft by the Golden State Warriors, where he has remained for his entire career.

Off the court, Curry is known for his philanthropy and business ventures. He has been involved in numerous charitable efforts, including his "Nothing But Nets" campaign to fight malaria and his Stephen and Ayesha Curry Family Foundation, which works on educational initiatives, empowering youth, and other humanitarian efforts. Curry is also an advocate for social justice and community development.

Stephen Curry's Family

Stephen Curry comes from a basketball family. His father, Dell Curry, is a former NBA player who played for the Charlotte Hornets, Cleveland Cavaliers, and Toronto Raptors. Dell was known for his exceptional three-point shooting, which undoubtedly influenced Stephen’s game.

Stephen’s mother, Sonya Curry, is a former college volleyball player, and both of his parents have played an integral role in his development as an athlete. Stephen’s younger brother, Seth Curry, is also an NBA player, currently with the Phoenix Suns, while his older brother, Sydel Curry, is a former volleyball player who has built a career in business and social media.

Stephen is married to Ayesha Curry, a chef, actress, and entrepreneur. The couple got married in 2011 and have three children: Riley, Ryan, and Canon. Ayesha has built a successful career in food and lifestyle with her brand, including TV shows, cookbooks, and a restaurant. The couple is very active in philanthropic work and frequently shares their family life with their fans on social media.

Endorsements & Business Ventures

Stephen Curry is one of the most marketable athletes globally, thanks to his success on the basketball court and his likable personality. Curry has lucrative endorsement deals with major brands such as Under Armour, which produces his popular "Curry" shoe line. His long-term deal with Under Armour is one of the most notable endorsements in the sports world. Curry has also worked with companies like Gatorade, Nissan, and Brita, among others.

Stephen Curry's Contracts & Earnings

Stephen Curry is one of the highest-paid athletes in the world, both in terms of his NBA salary and endorsements. He signed a 4-year, $215 million contract extension with the Golden State Warriors in 2021, making him the first player in NBA history to sign a contract worth over $200 million. This contract extension keeps him with the Warriors through the 2025-2026 season.

In addition to his NBA earnings, Curry is also heavily involved in endorsement deals that significantly boost his income. According to Forbes, Curry consistently ranks among the top-earning athletes, with his off-court ventures surpassing his basketball salary. His endorsement deals alone bring in tens of millions of dollars annually. He is also known for his savvy investments in tech startups and other emerging industries, which have further increased his wealth.

While Curry’s playing contract continues to grow, he has also been able to secure a long-term partnership with Under Armour, which is set to continue well into the 2020s. This agreement includes a significant portion of his earnings and has contributed to his overall financial success.

Achievements

  • 4× NBA Champion (2015, 2017, 2018, 2022)
  • 2× NBA MVP (2015, 2016 - Unanimous)
  • NBA Finals MVP (2022)
  • NBA All-Time Leader in Three-Point Shots Made
  • 9× NBA All-Star
  • NBA Scoring Champion (2016, 2021)
  • 2× Olympic Gold Medalist with Team USA

Career Milestones

  • Set the record for most three-pointers made in a season in 2015-2016 (402 three-pointers).
  • Led the Warriors to an NBA-best 73-9 regular season record in 2015-2016.
  • Became the first unanimous MVP in NBA history (2016).
  • Passed Ray Allen for the all-time three-point record in December 2021.
